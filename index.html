B-hadron Identification in Jets & Tracks using Novel Deep Learning technique in pp collisions in CMS

May 2024 - Present | Dr. Prabhat Pujahari IIT Madras, Dr. Matthew Nguyen, Dr. Shamik Ghosh, LLR, École Polytechnique

  • Working on b-jet tagging, classification, and clustering of tracks originating from b-hadron decays in pp collisions at CMS $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV, with a specific focus on studying the dead cone effect and other b-flavor physics.
  • Developed a novel approach utilizing geometric deep learning and point clouds to extract optimal b-hadron decay representations.
  • Achieved a significant 3-4% improvement in signal efficiency and background rejection compared to traditional BDTs on ParticleNet-tagged jets, by using a point cloud representation of jets composed solely of charged decay products.
  • Demonstrated strong performance with 0.97 AUC (ROC) and 92.7%+ accuracy for the graph b-jet classification task, and 0.965 AUC with 92.8% accuracy on the node-level track classification task, achieving results on par with ParticleNet & BDTs.
  • Implementing state-of-the-art loss functions and forming quantitative clustering measures for effective track separation.
  • Developed a comprehensive Python codebase from scratch, covering data reading, preprocessing, graph neural network architecture development, and training/testing pipelines for over 50 GB of pp collision data, alongside informative plots.
  • Built a robust pipeline to apply cuts and convert awkward arrays from ROOT pp collision files into dataframes for HDF5 storage, subsequently transforming them into state dictionary files compatible with model architectures for training and testing.
